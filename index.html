A short roadmap about my academic carrer

I am a Biochemist from the School of Pharmacy and Biochemistry at the University of Buenos Aires. I teach about new methodologies applied to precision medicine there. Currently, I work in the Molecular Biology Laboratory of the Translational Research Unit named GENUIT, placed at the Gastroenterology Hospital "Dr. C. B. Udaondo", in the Autonomous City of Buenos Aires with the virtuous support of IATTGI (www.iattgi.org). I developed my doctoral thesis (not yet defended) in collaboration with the Gastrointestinal and Pancreatic Translational Oncology Research Group at @idibaps @hospitalclinic @ciberehd. The aim of my thesis was to identify new predisposition genes for colorectal cancer in young patients with deficiencies in the DNA repair system. I used tools like germline and tumor exome sequencing, as well as in vitro techniques such as CRISPR/Cas9. Currently, I coordinate the translational research studies of the Unit, focused on discovering new biomarkers in gastrointestinal tumors that can predict the response to targeted treatments and, in turn, forecast their survival.

Personal Interest

Computational Cancer Research

Continuously seeking novel and cutting-edge algorithms and pipelines that can be applied to real-time patient decision-making.

Data Science and Machine Learning

Exploring how historical data can be leveraged to rationally predict current outcomes in a real-world context.

Gastrointestinal Cancer Predisposition

Searching new germline variants that could assist families in making informed decisions to prevent cancer within a public context.

Biomarkers in GI tumors

Unraveling how limited data from poorly preserved FFPE samples can contribute to making decisions regarding treatment prediction and survival prognosis.
